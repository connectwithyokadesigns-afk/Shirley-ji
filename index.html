First-Time Buyer

Your Complete Buyer Guide

Everything you need to know before making the biggest purchase of your life — explained simply.

01

Assess Your Readiness

Before you begin searching, understand your financial position. Review your credit score, savings, and monthly income to set realistic expectations.

Aim for a credit score of 620 or higher
Calculate your debt-to-income ratio (ideally under 43%)
Budget for closing costs (typically 2–5% of purchase price)
02

Get Pre-Approved

A pre-approval letter from a lender tells sellers you're a serious buyer and gives you a clear, firm budget to work with. It involves a credit check and document review.

Gather W-2s, pay stubs, and bank statements
Compare at least 3 lenders for the best rate
03

House Hunting

Create a prioritized list of must-haves vs. nice-to-haves. Visit open houses and private showings with your Shirley Ji advisor, who helps you see beyond staging.

Research neighborhoods, schools, and commutes
Look beyond cosmetics — check structure and systems
04

Making an Offer

We help you craft a competitive, strategic offer based on market data — not emotion. Understand contingencies, earnest money, and negotiation levers.

05

Inspection & Due Diligence

Never skip the inspection. A licensed inspector examines the home's structure, systems, and safety. Use findings to negotiate repairs or price adjustments.

06

Closing Day

Review all closing documents carefully. You'll sign the mortgage, pay closing costs, and receive your keys. Shirley Ji reviews every document with you before you sign.
